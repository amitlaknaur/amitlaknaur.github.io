What intrigues me about Product Management

Motto: Lead with the user need and know your value add.

                                               

My zeal for product management has been inherent in my work so far. As a lead engineer, I have been instrumental in creating a framework for developing test, measurement, validation and automation tools with higher than intended adoption in my forte of electrical engineering with a focus on my users. I strived for best End-User experience, usability, affordance,  and championed the associated cost, scalability, development backlogs, epics, and stories.

                                                     

As I transition into product management, I leverage my strengths around critical thinking and creative solutioning in the multidimensional journey of a product. I naturally gravitate to these core skills even in day to day setting. For example, we recently purchased a particular swing-chair of the shortlisted two, where-in the rejected one had a semicircular base for stability but matched the exact floor color which would be a tripping hazard. When I say this aloud in the store I think of analogous issues on the products I am/will-be handling. In term of work experience I have covered from market research, feature prioritization, iterative application devlopment, ROI analysis, product roadmap, and delivered super-and-simple products.

                                               

Using some stories mine and otherwise, let me draw a picture of how I connect to the product management/ownership arena. For example please look at the pictures below tell me which one is appealing the most. At a high level, they are all sunsets. The trees bring in the dimension depth and, anchor our perspective. The image pops up when the rule of thirds is used in framing the foreground to background ratio. We apply this ratio vertically too to place the trees within the right third of the frame. Just the way there is the science focus, lighting, and composition that goes into clicking a good picture, enough science and tools go into owning a product and molding it. These can only go so far, from there we need to engage with indirect tools from market analysis, competition research, User research, pricing, promotion, user demography and more to facilitate launching a product and reap success.
